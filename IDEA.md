# Aurora Study Coach - Idea Brief (Tightened)

## Problem
Rwandan secondary students in low-bandwidth areas often have limited access to quality explanations, practice, and personalized feedback. This slows exam readiness and widens the "innovation divide" where rural learners fall behind.

## Target Users
- Grade 10-12 students (starting with Grade 11)
- Low-end Android phones
- Intermittent or expensive mobile data
- Bilingual needs: Kinyarwanda + English

## SDG Alignment
- **SDG 4: Quality Education**
- Sub-goals: equitable access to learning resources, improved learning outcomes, and digital inclusion

## Solution (One-liner)
An offline-first, bilingual AI Study Coach that uses mastery learning and spaced repetition to deliver exam-focused practice and explanations, even without internet.

## Core Differentiators
- **Mastery-based learning**: students advance only when they show mastery, not just completion.
- **Spaced repetition**: schedules review scientifically to improve long-term retention.
- **RAG tutoring**: AI answers only from approved curriculum notes to reduce hallucinations.
- **Offline-first**: works on 2G/3G with cached packs and a sync queue.

## What Makes This “Best Student” Level
- Mistake diagnosis (why the student got it wrong)
- Adaptive question selection (review + new)
- Bilingual explanations with key terms in English
- Teacher-friendly analytics for weak topics

## MVP Scope (Hackathon)
- PWA with offline packs (Math + Physics)
- Quiz flow + immediate feedback
- Mastery + spaced repetition engine
- AI Tutor endpoints (explain, similar questions, mistake diagnosis)
- Sync queue and analytics endpoint

## Success Metrics
- 20%+ improvement in quiz scores after 2 sessions
- 70%+ mastery score for key topics
- Positive student feedback on clarity of Kinyarwanda explanations

## Validation Plan
- Pilot with 5-10 classmates
- Collect screenshots, scores, and short feedback quotes
- Record short demo video showing offline mode
